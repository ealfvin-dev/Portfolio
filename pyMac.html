PyMac

Developed data reduction software to calculate results for precision mass measurements. In metrology we disseminate mass starting at 1 kg, working down to 1 mg and up to 100,000 lb. Weighing designs are used to compare summations of masses and set up an outline for the linear system of equations that must be solved to calculate the mass of each item in the design. The software uses a least squares best fit matrix solution to calculate results from measurement data. An F and T test are performed to evaluate the standard deviation (residuals) of the best fit, and the calculated results of a known weight in the design against a reference value, respectively.

The program is written as a substitue for MassCode, developed by the Mass and Force group at the National Institute of Standards and Technology. PyMac offers additional features such as the ability to correct for air buoyancy on each line of measurements, the ability to correct for center of mass height differences given a local gravity gradient, and eventually a front end to make the program more user-friendly.
